Preamble

The purpose of this bill is to create a foundation for future environmental sustainability related legislation, including the creation of two federal cabinet level departments with roles related to this objective.

Article I: Findings and Purpose

Section 1

Congress finds that healthy, diverse, and productive communities of fish, wildlife, and plants provide significant benefits to the people and economy of the United States, including

  1. abundant clean water supplies;
  2. food and coastal storm protection;
  3. clean air;
  4. a source of food, fiber, medicines, and pollination of the crops and other plants of the United States;
  5. outdoor recreation, which is a source of jobs and economic stimulus;
  6. hunting and fishing opportunities and support for subsistence communities;
  7. opportunities for scientific research and education;
  8. world-class tourism destinations that support local economies; and
  9. sequestration and storage of carbon to help mitigate changes to the global climate system.

Section 2

This congress also recognises the natural and disastrous events that occur, and become more likely in the future because of climate change, are a direct threat to the biological sustainability and growth of this nation. These events include:

  1. severe droughts and heat waves;
  2. severe storms and floods;
  3. frequent and severe wildfires;
  4. more frequent and severe outbreaks of forest pests and invasive species;
  5. flooding and erosion of coastal areas due to rising sea levels;
  6. melting glaciers and sea ice;
  7. thawing permafrost;
  8. shifting distributions of fish, wildlife, and plant populations;
  9. disruptive shifts in the timing of fish, wildlife, and plant natural history cycles, such as blooming, breeding, and seasonal migrations;
  10. increasing ocean temperatures and acidification;
  11. altered patterns of rain, snow, runoff, and streamflow; and
  12. habitat loss, degradation, fragmentation, and movement.

Section 3

The Federal Government should provide leadership in preparing for and responding to the effects described in paragraph to ensure that present and future generations continue to receive the benefits of the abundant and diverse fish, wildlife, and plant resources of the United States. The policy of the federal government is to cooperate with state, local and tribal governments on this matter of environmental security and preservation, to the best of its ability.

Article II: Creation of the Department of the Interior

Section 1

Mandates for the creation of a cabinet level department, responsible for the management and conservation of most federal lands and natural resources, and the administration of programs relating to Native Americans, Alaska Natives, Native Hawaiians, territorial affairs, and insular areas of the United States, and designated federal lands.

Section 2

Funds for this cabinet level department will be set out by congress each year, with funds able to be temporary, recurring, mandatory or a one time payment. Funds can also be a mix of these 4 options. Article comes into effect immediately after signature of president.

Section 3

The role of Secretary of the Interior is created by this article. This does not have to be filled if the president desires.

Section 4

All related bills, if passed by congress and signed by the president, are under the oversight of the Secretary of the Interior, and the President.

Article III: Creation of the Department of Environmental Affairs

Section 1

Mandates for the creation of a cabinet level department, with the objective to grow and protect the environment, and the plants and animals that are within it, with the goal of future preservation.

Section 2

Section 3

The role of Secretary of Environmental Affairs is created by this article. This does not have to be filled if the president desires.

Section 4

All environmental related bills, if passed by congress and signed by the president, are under the oversight of the Secretary of the Environmental Affairs, and the President.

Article IV: Creation and Maintenance of National Parks

Section 1

Mandates for the creation of protected areas, called national parks, under the jurisdiction of the Department of the Interior. These National Parks can be grown by congress, the president, or the secretary of the interior. These parks are only to be created for the interest of sustainability, and for the protection of the flora and fauna in their respective areas. This article comes into effect immediately.

Article V: Dept. of Environmental Affairs Regulations.

Section 1

Mandates for the Department of Environmental Affairs, and the Secretary for Environmental Affairs, to create regulations that comply and help with the departments interest of environmental protection and sustainability.

Section 2

Regulations may also be created by Presidential Executive Order, or through Congressional Statute.

Section 3

Regulations may be challenged in a court of law, or an Dept. of Environmental Affairs economic sustainability awareness oversight panel, which deals with the issue of environmental regulations hurting business activity. Article comes into effect immediately.
