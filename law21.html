Stop Family Separation Act

Status Cosponsors Senate House Signatures

Preamble

This bill defines what an agent or contractor may do to illegal aliens entering the U.S. Mainland and does not refer to Overseas region in particular of minors who are under the age of 18 and separation from their parents.

Article I: Definitions

Section 1: What is meant by the term 'minor'?

A minor is anyone, illegal or legal, on US mainland who is under the legal age of 18. Other terms that are used may be ‘child’ or ‘children’ depending on the plurality of the context.

Section 2: Who are agents/contractors?

This refers to an employee, direct or indirect, of any of the following departments:

  • Department of Justice (DJ)
  • Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS)
  • Department of Homeland Security (DHS)

Article II: Prohibitions

Section 1: Fundamentals

All agents/contractors are prohibited from separating a minor who has no permanent immigration status, from his or her parent/guardian from any part of immigration zone i.e. 100 miles from the border or near the port of entry unless:

  • an authorised state court determines that it is in the child’s best interests to be removed;
  • a state or county child welfare official with expertise in child trauma and development determines that it is in the child’s best interests to be removed from his or her parents as a result of abuse or neglect; or
  • the Chief Patrol Agent or the Area Port Director authorizes separation based on the documented finding that the child is a trafficking victim or is at significant risk of becoming a victim, a strong likelihood exists that the adult is not the parent/guardian, or the child is in danger of abuse or neglect.

Agencies of DJ, DHHS, DHS are not permitted to remove a child from a parent/guardian solely for the policy goals of deterring migration to the United States or of promoting immigration law compliance.

Article III: Presumptions

Section 1: Unity

This bill is in favor of family and sibling UNITY and parental rights unless authorized personnel deem otherwise.

Section 2: Detention Centers

All DETENTION CENTRES or the likes of which they resemble this behaviour is not in the interest of families and children.
