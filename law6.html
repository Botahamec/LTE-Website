Universal Medicare for All Act

Preamble Article I Article II
Status Cosponsors House Senate Signatures

Preamble

Section 1: Introduction

As the country becomes increasingly inundated with sick citizens taken advantage of by corporations willing to do anything for a profit, this bill shall establish the single-payer Medicare for All Program to provide all individuals residing in the United States and U.S. territories with free health care that includes all medically necessary care. This includes primary care and prevention, dietary and nutritional therapies, prescription drugs, emergency care, long-term care, mental health services, dental services, and vision care. As an act made by and for the people, patients may choose from participating physicians and institutions. Health insurers may not sell health insurance that duplicates the benefits provided under this bill. Insurers may sell benefits that are not medically necessary, such as cosmetic surgery benefits. This bill also sets forth methods to pay institutional providers and health professionals for services. Financial incentives to health organizations and physicians based on utilization are prohibited. This bill will come into effect after it has been made into law and the economic simulator is established.

Section 2: Summary

This bill establishes a unique American universal health insurance program with single-payer financing. The bill would create a publicly financed, privately delivered health care system that improves and expands the already existing Medicare program to all U.S. residents, and all residents living in U.S. territories. The goal of the legislation is to ensure that all Americans will have access, guaranteed by law, to the highest quality and most cost effective health care services regardless of their employment, income or health care status. In short, health care becomes a fundamental human right, with no financial barriers or financial harm resulting from seeking care. With 42 million uninsured Americans, and many millions more who are underinsured, the time has come to change our inefficient and costly fragmented non-system of health care. The current for-profit health care system in the U.S. is not financially sustainable over the long run, hence the need for a unique nonprofit, universal single-payer health care system.

  1. Every eligible citizen receives a “Medicare For All Card” and ID number once they enroll at the appropriate location. Social Security numbers may not be used when assigning ID cards, as they can be easily used fraudulently.
  2. This program will cover all medically necessary services, therefore services such as cosmetic surgery are not covered. Patients have their choice of physicians, providers, hospitals, clinics, and practices, and there are no co-pays or deductibles allowed.
  3. Doctors, hospitals, and clinics will continue to operate as private entities. However, they will be unable to issue stock (excluding clinics not included, such as cosmetic surgery clinics). Private health insurers shall be prohibited under this act from selling coverage that duplicates the benefits of the Improved Medicare For All program.
  4. Currently, health care costs for a typical family of four in the most common health plan offered by employers are an average of $8,584 a year ( $5,114 in premiums and another $3,470 in out-of-pocket medical services, drugs, and supplies). Employers pay an additional $12,144 towards the coverage, for a total cost per family of $20,728. Fiscal studies by economists Dean Baker (2007) and Gerald Friedman (2013) have estimated that under the HR 676, which is closely modeled to the Universal Medicare for All Act, 95% percent of U.S. households would be paying less than they now for all health care costs. Most businesses would pay drastically less too.

Section 3: Dedication

This bill is dedicated by Representative John Conyers (D-MI), as he is the inspiration and ideological foundation this bill is based on. We will fight for the bill he made for the people and by the people, despite his version being killed by Congressional Republicans.

Article I: Eligibility and Benefits for Citizens

Section 1: Eligibility of Citizens

  1. In general, all individuals residing in the United States (including any territory of the United States) are covered under the Medicare For All Program entitling them to a universal, best quality standard of care.
  2. Each eligible individual shall receive a card with a unique number in the mail. An individual’s Social Security number shall not be used for purposes of registration under this section.
  3. Individuals and families shall receive a Medicare For All Program Card in the mail, after filling out a Medicare For All Program application form at a health care provider. Such application form shall be no more than 2 pages long.
  4. Individuals who present themselves for covered services from a participating provider shall be presumed to be eligible for benefits under this Act, but shall complete an application for benefits in order to receive a Medicare For All Program Card and have payment made for such benefits.
  5. The Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services shall create rules that provides criteria for determining residency for eligibility purposes under the Medicare For All Program.

Section 2: Benefits of the Medicare for All System

All citizens eligible for this program shall recieve:

  1. Primary care and prevention
  2. Approved dietary and nutritional therapies
  3. Inpatient care
  4. Outpatient care
  5. Emergency care
  6. Prescription drugs
  7. Durable medical equipment
  8. Long term care
  9. Palliative care
  10. Mental health services
  11. The full scope of dental services, services[sic], including periodontics, oral surgery, and endontics, but not including cosmetic dentistry
  12. Substance abuse treatment
  13. Basic vision care and vision correction (other than laser vision correction for cosmetic purposes).
  14. Hearing services, including coverage of hearing aids.
  15. Podiatric care
    1. These benefits are available through any licensed health care clinician anywhere in the United States that is legally qualified to provide the benefits.
    2. No deductibles, copayments, coinsurance, or other cost-sharing shall be imposed with respect to covered benefits.

Section 3: Qualifications of Health Care Providers Participating in the Medicare for All (MFA) Program

In order to qualify as a public or non-profit provider:

  1. No institution may be a participating provider unless it is a public or not-for-profit institution. Private physicians, private clinics, and private health care providers shall continue to operate as private entities, but are prohibited from being investor owned.
  2. For-profit providers that want to participate shall be required to convert to not-for-profit status.
  3. For-profit providers of care that convert to non-profit status shall remain privately owned and operated entities.
  4. The owners of such for-profit providers shall be compensated for reasonable financial losses incurred as a result of the conversion from for-profit to non-profit status.
  5. There are authorized to be appropriated compensation necessary, as described in the bill.
  6. The payments to owners of converting for-profit providers shall occur during a 10-year period, through the sale of bonds. Payment for conversions shall not be made for loss of business profits.
  7. The Department of Health and Human Services shall create rules to provide a way to further the timely, efficient, and feasible conversion of for-profit providers of care.

In order to qualify as a public or non-profit provider, the provider must meet these quality standards:

  1. Health care delivery facilities must meet state quality and licensing guidelines as a condition of participation under such program, including guidelines regarding safe staffing and quality of care.
  2. Participating clinicians must be licensed in their State of practice and meet the quality standards for their area of care. No clinician whose license is under suspension or who is under disciplinary action in any State may be a participating provider.

In order to quality, providers must also NOT:

  1. sell health insurance coverage that duplicates the benefits provided under this Act.
  2. construe this bill as prohibiting the sale of health insurance coverage for any additional benefits not covered by this Act (cosmetic surgery or other services and items that are not medically necessary)

Patients shall have free choice of participating physicians and other clinicians, hospitals, and inpatient care facilities.

Article II: Budget and Funding of Bill Initiatives

Section 1: Funding ffor Doctors and Providers

  1. The Medicare For All Program, through its regional offices, shall pay each registered provider, including hospitals, nursing homes, community or migrant health centers, home care agencies, or other institutional providers or prepaid group p
  2. The MFA Program will pay licensed physicians and other clinicians through:
    1. Fee for service payment (as stated in the bill).
    2. Salaried positions within group practices or non-profit health maintenance

Section 2: Funding for the MFA (Medicare for All) Program

There are appropriated to the Medicare For All Trust Fund amounts sufficient to carry out this Act from the following sources:

  1. Existing sources of Federal Government revenues for health care.
  2. Increasing personal income taxes by five percent on the top 5 percent income earners (approximately all Americans making $225,000 a year or more).
  3. Instituting a modest tax of 2 percent on unearned income.

Other miscellaneous sources of funding:

  1. vastly reducing paperwork;
  2. requiring a rational bulk attainment of prescription drugs
  3. improved access to health care.

Amounts that would have been appropriated for federal public health care programs, including Medicare, Medicaid, and the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP), are transferred and appropriated to carry out this bill, as this bill supersedes much of the work done by these programs.
