Preamble

Firearms have and will continue to become increasingly inundated in our lives with proper and prompt firearm reformation. This bill will accomplish three main tasks:

  1. Once and for all close the gun show loophole that has lead to the illicit procurement of dangerous firearms. This will be accomplished by ensuring that:
    1. Anybody who is under the age of 21 cannot operate a gun show.
    2. Anybody who has been banned from shipping/receiving firearms cannot operate a gun show.
    3. All gun show operators must be registered with the Department of Justice (DoJ) database with photographs and fingerprints for identification.
    4. Anybody who has fabricated/concealed information in connection to gun show registration cannot operate a gun show.
    5. All gun show operators must notify the DoJ of the specific date, time and duration of any gun shows no later than 30 days prior to the show.
    6. It is unlawful to transfer the possession of a firearm at a gun show to another person not licensed unless a licensed importer, manufacturer, or dealer has, among other conditions, recorded the transfer with DOJ.
    7. The licensed dealer must notify the prospective transferor and transferee of the firearm that the national instant criminal background check system has provided the dealer with a unique identification number indicating that the receipt of the firearm would not violate certain federal or state firearm laws.
    8. The DOJ may enter without a warrant into any gun show, as they have reasonable cause, since the DOJ is examining records/firearm inventory.
  2. Expand and make background checks more efficient. This will be accomplished by:
    1. Cleaning up the language in US Federal Code to prevent loopholes and technicalities.
    2. The creation of a DOJ and Department of Trade interagency council, the Firearms Background Check Expansion Commission, which will create many jobs.
  3. Prohibiting the sale, manufacture, transport, or posses a semiautomatic assault weapon (SAW) and bump stocks, barring antique guns, possession of these guns is legal for executive department officials for work purposes only, and all other exemptions listed in the bill.
    1. Police and DOJ enforcement.
    2. Improved serial number identification system.

This bill will go into effect after the passing of it.

Article I: Gun Show Loophole Closure

Section 1: Gun Show Operator Precautions

This bill makes it unlawful for any person to operate a gun show unless such person:

  1. has attained 21 years of age
  2. is not prohibited from transporting, shipping, or receiving firearms and has not violated any federal firearms requirements
  3. has registered with the Department of Justice (DOJ) as a gun show operator and has provided a photograph and fingerprints
  4. has not concealed material information nor made false statements in connection with a gun show operator registration
  5. notifies DOJ of the date, time, and duration of a gun show not later than 30 days before the commencement of such show and verifies the identity of each vendor at the gun show.

Section 2: Prohibition of Gun Show Firearm Sales

The bill makes it unlawful for a person not licensed under this bill to transfer possession of a firearm at a gun show to another person not licensed unless a licensed importer, manufacturer, or dealer has, among other conditions, recorded the transfer with DOJ.

Additionally, the licensed dealer must notify the prospective transferor and transferee of the firearm that the national instant criminal background check system has provided the dealer with a unique identification number indicating that the receipt of the firearm would not violate certain federal or state firearm laws. The bill grants DOJ authority to enter, without a showing of a warrant, any place where a gun show is held or where a gun show operator is required to maintain records to examine records and inventory to determine compliance with this bill (reasonable cause).

Article II: Expansion and Optimization of the Background Check System

Section 1: Fixing Sloppy Language in Previous Bills

The following changes to 18 U.S. Code § 922:

  1. inserting “or ammunition” after “ firearm” each place it appears except in paragraph (3);

    Example: [Illegal for sale of] any firearm or ammunition to any person other than a licensed importer, licensed manufacturer, licensed dealer, or licensed collector.

  2. Edit paragraph 1A so that it adds:

    It shall be unlawful if the firearm transfer such other person has presented to the licensee a permit that—allows such other person to possess or acquire a firearm; and was issued not more than 5 years earlier by the State in which the transfer is to take place; and the law of the State provides that such a permit is to be issued only after an authorized government official has verified that the information available to such official does not indicate that possession of a firearm by such other person would be in violation of law.

  3. Edit paragraph 1B so that it also adds:

    It shall also be unlawful in the case of an ammunition transfer such other person has presented to the licensee a permit that—allows such other person to possess or acquire a firearm; and was issued not more than 5 years earlier by the State in which the transfer is to take place; and the law of the State provides that such a permit is to be issued only after an authorized government official has verified that the information available to such official does not indicate that possession of a firearm by such other person would be in violation of law.

Section 2: Creation of the Firearms Background Checks Commission (FBCC)

The DOJ and DOT shall work jointly in an interagency council, the FBCC, to enforce background check regulation laws and to ensure that gun dealers are fully complying with 100% of the necessary background checks. The FBCC also shall have the power to utilize rulemaking to expand upon Congressional mandates, in order to prevent the illicit procurement of firearms.

The FBCC will investigate and routinely check all gun dealership records once a year to ensure that firearm dealers are fully complying with the background check laws.

The FBCC may also check to see if any SAWs are sold, and if found guilty, the FBCC may fine and find violators responsible.

Article III: Semiautomatic Assault Weapons (SAWs) Ban

Section 1: Closing Loopholes in US Federal Code and Clarifications

Section 921(a) of title 18, United States Code, is amended:

  1. The term ‘semi-automatic pistol’ means any repeating pistol that—
    1. utilizes a portion of the energy of a firing cartridge to extract the fired cartridge case and chamber the next round; and
    2. requires a separate pull of the trigger to fire each cartridge.
  2. The term ‘semiautomatic shotgun’ means any repeating shotgun that—
    1. utilizes a portion of the energy of a firing cartridge to extract the fired cartridge case and chamber the next round; and
    2. requires a separate pull of the trigger to fire each cartridge. ; and

Add the following clarifications:

  1. The term ‘semiautomatic assault weapon’ means any of the following, regardless of country of manufacture or caliber of ammunition accepted:
    1. A semiautomatic rifle that has the capacity to accept a detachable magazine and any one of the following:
      1. A pistol grip
      2. A forward grip
      3. A folding, telescoping, or detachable stock.
      4. A grenade launcher or rocket launcher.
      5. A barrel shroud
      6. A threaded barrel
    2. A semiautomatic rifle that has a fixed magazine with the capacity to accept more than 10 rounds, except for an attached tubular device designed to accept, and capable of operating only with, .22 caliber rimfire ammunition.
    3. Any part, combination of parts, component, device, attachment, or accessory that is designed or functions to accelerate the rate of fire of a semiautomatic rifle but not convert the semi automatic rifle into a machine gun.
    4. A semiautomatic pistol that has the capacity to accept a detachable magazine and any one of the following:
      1. A threaded barrel.
      2. A second pistol grip.
      3. A barrel shroud
      4. The capacity to accept a detachable magazine at some location outside of the pistol grip.
      5. A semiautomatic version of an automatic firearm.
    5. A semiautomatic pistol with a fixed magazine that has the capacity to accept more than 10 rounds.
    6. A semiautomatic shotgun that has any one of the following:
      1. A folding, telescoping, or detachable stock.
      2. A pistol grip.
      3. A fixed magazine with the capacity to accept more than 5 rounds.
      4. The ability to accept a detachable magazine.
      5. A forward grip.
      6. A grenade launcher or rocket launcher.
    7. Any shotgun with a revolving cylinder.
    8. All of the following rifles, copies, duplicates, variants, or altered facsimiles with the capability of any such weapon thereof:
      1. All AK types, including the following:
        1. AK, AK47, AK47S, AK–74, AKM, AKS, ARM, MAK 90, MISR, NHM90, NHM91, Rock River Arms LAR–47, SA 85, SA93, Vector Arms AK–47, VEPR, WASR–10, and WUM.
        2. IZHMASH Saiga AK.
        3. MAADI AK47 and ARM.
        4. Norinco 56S, 56S2, 84S, and 86S.
        5. Poly Technologies AK47 and AKS.
      2. All AR types.

Section 922 of title 18, United States Code, is amended once more—

  1. by inserting after subsection (u) the following:
      1. It shall be unlawful for a person to import, sell, manufacture, transfer, or possess, in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce, a semiautomatic assault weapon or a bump stock.
      2. Paragraph (1) shall not apply to the possession, sale, or transfer of any semiautomatic assault weapon otherwise lawfully possessed under Federal law on the date of enactment of the Assault Weapons Ban of 2018.
      3. Paragraph (1) shall not apply to any firearm that—
        1. is manually operated by bolt, pump, lever, or slide action;
        2. has been rendered permanently inoperable; or
        3. is an antique firearm, as defined in section 921 of this title.
      4. Paragraph (1) shall not apply to—
        1. the importation for, manufacture for, sale to, transfer to, or possession by the United States or a department or agency of the United States or a State or a department, agency, or political subdivision of a State, or a sale or transfer to or possession by a qualified law enforcement officer employed by the United States or a department or agency of the United States or a State or a department, agency, or political subdivision of a State, for purposes of law enforcement (whether on or off duty), or a sale or transfer to or possession by a campus law enforcement officer for purposes of law enforcement (whether on or off duty);
        2. the importation for, or sale or transfer to a licensee under title I of the Atomic Energy Act of 1954 for purposes of establishing and maintaining an on-site physical protection system and security organization required by Federal law, or possession by an employee or contractor of such licensee on-site for such purposes or off-site for purposes of licensee-authorized training or transportation of nuclear materials;

Section 2: FBCC/DOJ Enforcement and Serial Number System Enforcement

The FBCC and DOJ shall have the power to carry out the Firearms Serial Number System (FSNS), which requires all firearm and ammunition manufacturers to register and label all firearms clearly with the FBCC. The FBCC has the authority to penalize all guilty manufacturers and will be given rulemaking powers to establish specifics, as outlined in this bill.
